Our proprietary model is engineered to capture risks that are unique to the world's largest cryptocurrency.

Part of what makes risk a hazard is its multi-faceted nature. Our comprehensive risk index is a meticulous blend of factors that serves to paint the picture in its multivariate splendour. We know that trading can be a balancing act between complexity and practicality. That's why, we compute the BCRI in realtime, delivering multidimensional insight that's packaged into a single, convenient variable.









Speculation Multiplier (S)

Economic theory reasons speculation—the belief in future price appreciation—to be one of the primary drivers of trading activity. Our researchers have designed the Speculation Multiplier as a means to track the balance in aggregate behaviour. A high S indicates an increase in speculative demand whereas a lower values suggests mounting payment demand for Bitcoin.

Centralisation Quotient (C)

We’ve developed the centralisation quotient for the express purpose of tracking the risk of de facto centralisation in Bitcoin infrastructure. A C of 0 is indicative of a fully decentralised network. In contrast a high quotient suggests that all infrastructural activity in a rolling 24-hour window was relayed by a single entity.

Cost Coefficient (K)

This is a metric that responds to the network’s collective reluctance towards administering transactions. The primary contributor to this factor is the resistance or cost that counterparties face when trying to make transactions. For example, the hypothetical ‘frictionless market’ would be characterised by a K of zero.

Index (BCRI)

We compute and publish the index in real-time, delivering multi-dimensional insight presented in a single, convenient variable. The indexing methodology is based around a modified inverse-variance weighting scheme of the three factors. It is calibrated at inception such that 100 represents the all-time trailing average.
